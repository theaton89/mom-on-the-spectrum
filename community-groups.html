Life as an autistic adult can feel lonely and isolating. We experience many things that others overlook and may spend a great deal of time feeling misunderstood and unsupported. Autistic Community Groups are designed to give autistic adults an opportunity to interact with a small group of other autistic adults over topics of shared interest, such as masking, seeking diagnosis, meltdowns, and more.


ACG's meet at several different times throughout the week in order to accommodate different time zones and schedules. Group time consists of brief check-ins where members are given the opportunity to share life updates with others (though it is not mandatory), followed by topic discussion. Taylor Heaton (Mom on the Spectrum) will introduce the topic and share educational information, then present questions to the group to encourage discussion and feedback. Discussion topics will rotate weekly and may coincide with the video content from Mom on the Spectrum.
